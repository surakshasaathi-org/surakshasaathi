2.2 PM-JAY — Deep Dive

Scheme identity

Official name

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY)

Launched

September 23, 2018 by PM Narendra Modi in Ranchi, Jharkhand

Governing body

National Health Authority (NHA), Ministry of Health and Family Welfare

Scale

World's largest government-funded health insurance scheme

Coverage amount

Rs.5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation

No. of beneficiaries

12 crore families (~55 crore individuals)

Hospital network

31,140 empanelled hospitals (public and private) as of Feb 2025

Procedures covered

1,900+ including 874 packages across 27 specialities

Pre-existing diseases

Covered from Day 1. No waiting period.

Family size cap

No cap on family size or age. All members covered under one card.

Portability

Pan-India. Use the Ayushman card at any empanelled hospital in any state.

Eligibility criteria (Rural households -- SECC 2011 deprivation categories)

D1: Only one room with kucha walls and kucha roof

D2: No adult member aged 16-59 years in household

D3: Female-headed household with no adult male 16-59 years

D4: Household with a disabled member and no able-bodied adult member

D5: SC/ST households

D7: Landless households deriving major income from manual casual labour

Auto-included: Destitute/living on alms, primitive tribal groups, legally released bonded labourers, manual scavengers

Eligibility criteria (Urban households -- 11 occupational categories)

Rag pickers, domestic workers, street vendors/cobblers

Construction workers, plumbers, masons, painters, welders, security guards, porters

Sweepers, sanitation workers, gardeners

Home-based workers, artisans, handicraft workers

Transport workers, rickshaw/auto drivers, cart pullers, conductors

Shop assistants, delivery workers, waiters, peons in small establishments

Electricians, mechanics, assemblers, repair workers

Washermen, chowkidars

Beggars (on alms)

Street vendors, hawkers

All other service providers (unspecified)

2024 Expansion -- Senior Citizens (70+)

From October 29, 2024: ALL Indians aged 70 and above are eligible regardless of income, occupation, or SECC status

Separate card issued under Ayushman Vay Vandana scheme, linked to PM-JAY infrastructure

Additional Rs.5 lakh cover exclusively for the senior citizen -- does not merge with family floater

Senior citizens already under CGHS, ECHS, or Ayushman CAPF must choose between their existing scheme and PM-JAY

Senior citizens with private health insurance can stack PM-JAY on top

47 lakh Vaya Vandana cards issued by February 2025

How to use PM-JAY at hospital (step-by-step)

Step 1: Visit any PM-JAY empanelled hospital. Public hospitals are automatically empanelled. Private hospitals: check the list on pmjay.gov.in or beneficiary.nha.gov.in.

Step 2: Go to the Ayushman Mitra desk (present at all empanelled hospitals). Show your Ayushman card (digital on phone is valid -- no print needed).

Step 3: Ayushman Mitra scans the QR code on the card OR looks up the family in the hospital portal using PMJAY ID or Aadhaar.

Step 4: Biometric verification (fingerprint or OTP on Aadhaar-linked mobile). This confirms identity and creates the admission record.

Step 5: Hospital generates a pre-authorisation request to the State Health Agency (SHA) for the procedure.

Step 6: SHA approves (for planned procedures) or auto-approves (for emergencies within 1 hour per IRDAI mandate).

Step 7: Treatment proceeds. All bills are settled directly between the hospital and SHA. Patient pays Rs.0.

Step 8: Post-discharge, hospital submits claim. SHA pays within 15 days.

What is covered (treatment packages)

Cancer: chemotherapy, surgery, radiation, bone marrow transplant

Cardiac: bypass surgery (CABG), angioplasty, stent insertion, heart valve replacement, pacemaker

Neurosurgery: brain tumour surgery, stroke treatment, Parkinson's care

Orthopaedics: hip and knee replacement, spinal fusion

Organ transplants: kidney, liver, heart, lung

Dialysis: covered for renal failure patients

Neonatal and paediatric care: newborn intensive care, congenital conditions

Burns: management of major burns

Urology: prostate, kidney stone procedures

Ophthalmology: cataract, glaucoma

Pre-hospitalisation: 3 days before admission

Post-hospitalisation: 15 days after discharge

Day-care procedures: included for surgeries not requiring overnight stay

⚠  NOT covered by base PM-JAY: OPD (outpatient) consultations, medicine for chronic diseases without hospitalisation, cosmetic procedures, fertility treatments, drug abuse treatment, self-inflicted injury, war injuries.

2.3 PMJJBY -- Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana

Scheme identity

Launched

May 9, 2015 by PM Modi in Kolkata

Administered by

LIC and other life insurance companies, through participating banks and post offices

Ministry

Ministry of Finance, Department of Financial Services

Coverage

Rs.2 lakh death benefit for death from ANY cause (natural, accidental, disease)

Premium

Rs.436 per year (auto-debited from bank account on or before May 31)

Policy period

June 1 to May 31 (annual, renewable)

Eligibility

Age 18-50 years with any bank account (join before 50; continue cover up to age 55)

Enrollment

23.63 crore enrolled as of April 2025

Total claims paid

Rs.18,397.92 crore for 9,19,896 families (inception to April 2025)

Critical issue: no policy document

⚠  PMJJBY issues no physical policy document. Banks just debit Rs.436 and provide an acknowledgment slip. This is the primary reason why lakhs of families do not know their deceased member had PMJJBY coverage. SurakshaSaathi's value: helping families discover and file these unclaimed claims.

Claim process -- death of insured person

Step 1: Nominee informs the bank branch where the insured had their account

Step 2: Nominee obtains the claim form from the bank branch (or download from jansuraksha.gov.in/Forms-PMJJBY.aspx)

Step 3: Fill and submit form with documents to the SAME bank branch within 30 days of death

Step 4: Bank verifies and forwards claim to the insurance company (LIC or partner insurer)

Step 5: Settlement in 1-2 weeks for clean cases. Amount credited directly to nominee's account.

Documents required for PMJJBY death claim

Completed PMJJBY claim form (available at bank branch or jansuraksha.gov.in)

Death certificate (original or certified copy)

Discharge slip from hospital (if death occurred in hospital)

Bank premium statement -- proof that Rs.436 was debited and policy was active at time of death

Cancelled cheque of nominee's bank account (for payment)

Aadhaar of deceased and nominee

Proof of relationship between nominee and deceased (ration card, birth certificate, marriage certificate)

⚠  If no nominee was registered: legal heirs must go through a separate legal process (succession certificate or indemnity bond) which takes significantly longer. SurakshaSaathi should proactively alert enrolled users to add and verify nominees.

Post Office route (DoP / CPRC)

Claims for PMJJBY via post office accounts route through the Centralised Processing and Reconciliation Centre (CPRC), Chennai 600002

Divisional Heads verify claim completeness before forwarding to CPRC

Checklist (DoP October 2025): Death certificate, Aadhaar of deceased and nominee, bank proof of nominee (cancelled cheque -- not POSB/IPPB), premium deduction ledger, proof of relationship

4.2 Hospital Refusal Escalation Script

This is the highest-distress scenario. A user is at a hospital, the hospital is refusing to honor PM-JAY, and the family may be in a medical crisis. The platform must have an emergency-mode script for this.

Immediate: Call 14555 (toll-free, 24x7). Tell them: hospital name, city, PMJAY ID, and that the hospital is refusing cashless treatment.

At the hospital: demand to speak with the hospital's PM-JAY nodal officer (all empanelled hospitals must have one per NHA mandate)

Document in writing: get the refusal in writing from the hospital with reason stated. This is required for CGRMS filing.

File CGRMS: cgrms.pmjay.gov.in. Select state, enter hospital name, PMJAY ID, describe issue. Gets a tracking number and SMS.

If life-threatening emergency: under IRDAI 2025 mandate, cashless authorization must be issued within 1 hour. If not, insurer is liable for additional costs.

Consumer court option: if scheme amount is under Rs.50 lakh, file with Insurance Ombudsman after scheme grievance fails.

4.3 Unclaimed PMJJBY/PMSBY Recovery Flow

ℹ  An estimated significant proportion of PMJJBY death benefits are never claimed because: (1) no policy document is issued, (2) nominee may not know, (3) claim must be filed within 30 days (hard for bereaved families), (4) complex documentation.

Step 1: Check if deceased had a savings account in a participating bank (most public and private banks participate)

Step 2: Request last 2 years bank statements from the bank. Look for annual debit of Rs.436 (PMJJBY) or Rs.20 (PMSBY) -- typically in May/June each year.

Step 3: If deduction is found, the policy was active. Request from bank: the insurance company name and policy reference number (the bank issues this upon enrollment).

Step 4: Download claim form from jansuraksha.gov.in. Fill in nominee/legal heir details. Attach all documents.

Step 5: Submit to the SAME bank where premium was deducted. Do not submit to the insurance company directly -- the bank is the interface.

Step 6: If nominee was not registered: get legal heir certificate or succession certificate from local court. File with bank along with indemnity bond.

Step 7: Follow up within 2-4 weeks. If unresolved, escalate to CPGRAMS or Banking Ombudsman (IRDAI Banking Ombudsman handles disputes related to bank insurance product administration).

5.2 API and Integration Landscape

ABDM APIs (Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission)

ABHA creation API: create Ayushman Bharat Health Account (14-digit health ID) for users. Registration at abdm.gov.in.

Health Records API: fetch patient health records linked to ABHA ID (with patient consent)

PMJAY eligibility API: check PM-JAY eligibility using Aadhaar number or mobile number. Requires NHA partner registration.

Sandbox environment: sandbox.abdm.gov.in -- test APIs without real data. NHA provides developer access upon application.

API documentation: developer.nha.gov.in or abdm.gov.in/developer

Auth model: OAuth 2.0 with Aadhaar OTP-based authentication for beneficiary consent

Data sources for scheme eligibility database (non-API)

SECC 2011 data: not publicly available as raw API; eligibility determination is done through the PM-JAY portal/app

National Food Security Act (NFSA) data: used by some states for expanded PM-JAY eligibility

State-specific APIs: state SHAs may have their own APIs for scheme eligibility. Must be negotiated state by state.

MyScheme portal: provides scheme metadata (coverage, eligibility, links) in structured format. Can be scraped/integrated.

Hospital network data

PM-JAY empanelled hospital list: available as a searchable tool on pmjay.gov.in/hospitals. Downloadable as Excel/CSV from NHA on request.

Updates: hospital list is dynamic (hospitals can be de-empanelled for fraud). As of Feb 2025, 1,114 hospitals de-empanelled.

State SHA APIs: some states expose hospital-level APIs for empanelment status. Example: NHA's Hospital Empanelment Module (HEM 2.0) data is queryable through NHA partners.

⚠  SurakshaSaathi must NOT cache the empanelled hospital list for more than 24 hours. Hospitals are routinely de-empanelled for fraud (549 suspended and 1,114 de-empanelled as of early 2025). A user going to a de-empanelled hospital based on stale data will face denial of services.

7.1 Eligibility Engine Logic

Input variables to collect from user

State of residence (dropdown with all 28 states + 8 UTs)

Type of scheme checking: government health (PM-JAY), life (PMJJBY), accident (PMSBY), employer (ESIC), central govt employee (CGHS)

Age of person to be covered (triggers senior citizen 70+ auto-PM-JAY)

Household income (rough self-declaration: below Rs.1.2L / Rs.1.2-5L / above Rs.5L)

Occupation category (for urban PM-JAY eligibility)

Bank account status (yes/no) -- required for PMJJBY and PMSBY

Employer type (government central/state, private company with 10+ employees, self-employed, informal) -- determines ESIC and CGHS eligibility

Monthly salary (below Rs.21,000 = ESIC eligible for private sector)

Output: entitlement stack

For each eligible scheme: scheme name, coverage amount, next step, link to enrollment or hospital

For PM-JAY specifically: "Here is how to check if your name is in the database" (cannot confirm without database lookup)

For PMJJBY/PMSBY: "If you have a bank account, you can enroll right now. Here is how."

For ESIC: "Your employer must register you. If they have not, here are your rights."

Edge cases to handle

User is in West Bengal: PM-JAY portability does not apply; redirect to Swasthya Sathi

User is 70+ years: auto-suggest PM-JAY senior citizen enrollment regardless of income

User is CGHS eligible but does not know: ask if family member is/was central government employee or pensioner

User has private insurance: PM-JAY can be stacked on top (for PM-JAY eligible users). PMSBY/PMJJBY can also be stacked.

User is migrant worker in Kerala: flag Awaz Health Insurance Scheme specifically

7.3 Common Failure Points to Handle in UX

Aadhaar not linked to mobile: guide to update Aadhaar mobile at nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra or via mAadhaar app

Name spelling mismatch (Aadhaar vs SECC data): guide to check both spellings, try alternate searches, or contact DGNO for database correction

Migration (family moved states): PM-JAY is portable nationally for treatment. But eligibility record stays in original state. Guide to use 14555 for cross-state issues.

Death before claim filed (PMJJBY 30-day window missed): guide to file with bank explaining the delay. Banks have discretion. Also try Banking Ombudsman for unresolved cases.

Hospital is newly de-empanelled but user doesn't know: always check hospital status in real-time from pmjay.gov.in/hospitals before recommending. Never use cached data.

Senior citizen does not have active mobile number linked to Aadhaar: in-person CSC enrollment is the alternative. Guide to CSC locator: locator.csccloud.in

ESIC employee says employer did not register them: this is a violation of ESI Act. Guide to file complaint at ESIC office or through shramsuvidha.gov.in

7.4 Scheme Database Update Protocol

Scheme coverage amounts are revised periodically (e.g., Meghalaya increased coverage in recent update)

New states join PM-JAY (Delhi joined April 2025 -- this changed the eligibility logic for Delhi residents)

Scheme names change (YSR Aarogyasri became NTR Vaidya Seva Trust in 2024)

New eligibility categories added (37 lakh ASHA/Anganwadi workers added in March 2024)

Hospital de-empanelment updates: NHA de-empanels hospitals routinely for fraud

Recommended update triggers:

Monthly: check NHA press releases and state SHA websites for scheme updates

Daily: refresh empanelled hospital count from pmjay.gov.in API

Event-driven: subscribe to NHA email alerts and PIB RSS feed for scheme change announcements
